*** Problem Statement ***

Let $n$ be a positive integer. A polynomial $P \in \mathbb{Z}[x]$ is called **$n$-good** if there exists a quadratic polynomial $Q \in \mathbb{Z}[x]$ such that
$$
Q(k)(P(k) + Q(k)) \not\equiv 0 \pmod{n}
$$
for all integers $k$. Determine all integers $n$ such that **every** integer-coefficient polynomial is $n$-good.
