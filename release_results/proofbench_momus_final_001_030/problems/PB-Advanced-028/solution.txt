**Part 2: The Final Synthesis**

**The Architect's Log (Hemingway Style):**
We chose the ground at the foot of the altitude. $D$ was the origin. The mountain $A$ sat on the vertical. $B$ and $C$ were on the flat earth. **The Final Blueprint:**

**Problem:**
Let $H$ be the orthocenter of acute triangle $ABC$, let $F$ be the foot of the altitude from $C$ to $AB$, and let $P$ be the reflection of $H$ across $BC$. Suppose that the circumcircle of triangle $AFP$ intersects line $BC$ at two distinct points $X$ and $Y$. Prove that $C$ is the midpoint of $XY$.

**Proof:**
1.  **Coordinate System:** Let $D$ be the foot of the altitude from $A$ to $BC$. Define $D$ as the origin $(0,0)$. Let the line $BC$ lie on the $x$-axis and the line $AD$ lie on the $y$-axis.
    *   $A = (0, a)$
    *   $B = (b, 0)$
    *   $C = (c, 0)$
    Since $\triangle ABC$ is acute, $D$ lies between $B$ and $C$, implying $b$ and $c$ have opposite signs. We set $b < 0 < c$.

2.  **Coordinates of $H$ and $P$:**
    The altitude from $A$ is the line $x=0$. The line $AC$ has slope $m_{AC} = \frac{0-a}{c-0} = -a/c$. The altitude from $B$ to $AC$ is perpendicular to $AC$, so its slope is $c/a$. The equation of this altitude is:
    $y - 0 = \frac{c}{a}(x - b)$.
    The orthocenter $H$ is the intersection of $x=0$ and this altitude. Substituting $x=0$ yields $y = -bc/a$. Thus, $H = (0, -bc/a)$.
    Point $P$ is the reflection of $H$ across the $x$-axis ($BC$), so $P = (0, bc/a)$.

3.  **Coordinates of $F$:**
    The line $AB$ has slope $m_{AB} = \frac{0-a}{b-0} = -a/b$. Its equation is $y = -\frac{a}{b}x + a$.
    The line $CF$ is the altitude from $C$ to $AB$, so its slope is $b/a$. Its equation is $y = \frac{b}{a}(x - c)$.
    To find $F(x_F, y_F)$, we equate the two expressions for $y$:
    $-\frac{a}{b}x + a = \frac{b}{a}x - \frac{bc}{a}$.
    Multiplying by $ab$ gives $-a^2x + a^2b = b^2x - b^2c$, so $x(a^2 + b^2) = a^2b + b^2c$.
    $x_F = \frac{b(a^2+bc)}{a^2+b^2}$ and $y_F = \frac{b}{a}(x_F - c) = \frac{b}{a}(\frac{a^2b + b^2c - a^2c - b^2c}{a^2+b^2}) = \frac{ab(b-c)}{a^2+b^2}$.

4.  **Circumcenter $O$ of $\triangle AFP$:**
    Let $O = (x_O, y_O)$. Since $A(0, a)$ and $P(0, bc/a)$ lie on the $y$-axis, $O$ lies on their perpendicular bisector:
    $y_O = \frac{a + bc/a}{2} = \frac{a^2 + bc}{2a}$.
    $O$ also lies on the perpendicular bisector of $AF$. The midpoint of $AF$ is $M = (\frac{x_F}{2}, \frac{a+y_F}{2})$. Since the slope of $AF$ is $-a/b$, the slope of the bisector is $b/a$. The equation is:
    $y - \frac{a+y_F}{2} = \frac{b}{a}(x - \frac{x_F}{2})$.
    Substitute $y_O$ into the equation to find $x_O$:
    $\frac{a^2+bc}{2a} - \frac{a+y_F}{2} = \frac{b}{a}(x_O - \frac{x_F}{2})$
    $\frac{bc - ay_F}{2a} = \frac{b}{a}(x_O - \frac{x_F}{2})$.
    Multiply by $a/b$:
    $x_O = \frac{bc - ay_F}{2b} + \frac{x_F}{2} = \frac{bc - ay_F + bx_F}{2b}$.
    Substitute the expressions for $y_F$ and $x_F$:
    $bc - ay_F + bx_F = bc - \frac{a^2b(b-c)}{a^2+b^2} + \frac{b^2(a^2+bc)}{a^2+b^2} = \frac{bc(a^2+b^2) - a^2b^2 + a^2bc + a^2b^2 + b^3c}{a^2+b^2}$
    $= \frac{a^2bc + b^3c + a^2bc + b^3c}{a^2+b^2} = \frac{2bc(a^2+b^2)}{a^2+b^2} = 2bc$.
    Thus, $x_O = \frac{2bc}{2b} = c$.

5.  **Midpoint Property:**
    The circumcircle of $\triangle AFP$ has center $O(c, y_O)$. The line $BC$ is the $x$-axis ($y=0$). The points $X$ and $Y$ are the intersections of the circle and the line $y=0$. For any circle, the perpendicular projection of the center onto a chord lies at the midpoint of that chord. The projection of $O(c, y_O)$ onto the $x$-axis is the point $(c, 0)$, which is vertex $C$. Therefore, $C$ is the midpoint of $XY$.
