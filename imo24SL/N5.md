*** Problem Statement ***

Let $S$ be a finite nonempty set of prime numbers. Let $1 = b_1 < b_2 < \cdots$ be the sequence of all positive integers whose prime divisors all belong to $S$. Prove that for all but finitely many $n$, there exist positive integers $a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n$ such that
$$
\frac{a_1}{b_1} + \frac{a_2}{b_2} + \cdots + \frac{a_n}{b_n} = \frac{1}{b_1} + \frac{1}{b_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{b_n}.
$$
